Workshop structure

The following is the workshop agenda. Each section combines theory — methodology, best practices, frameworks, and tips — with a hands-on participant activity applied to the selected topic or question.

I bring prebuilt prompts, skills and workflows into every session. Participants spend the time customizing them to their domain — not building from scratch. That's what makes this work in a few hours or a day.

Frame the decision, formulate the intelligence questions

Turn a vague competitive concern into a bounded, answerable intelligence assignment

Topics

  • Name the decision the work must inform
  • Identify assumptions driving the decision
  • Turn assumptions into answerable intelligence questions
  • Focus scope and lock definitions
  • Set the evidence bar and define "done"

AI angle

  • Expose hidden assumptions and alternative framings
  • Sharpen questions
  • Get critique
  • Model plan before model runs

Hands-on activity

  • Bring your own decision / question
  • Identify assumptions most likely to change the decision
  • Turn assumptions into questions and a plan
  • Set scope, evidence standards, and a stopping condition

Working output

Decision statementIntelligence questions (prioritized)Scope & evidence barInitial research brief

Design and execute the collection plan

Decide what evidence is needed, where it may exist, and how to collect it in a traceable way

Topics

  • Map available sources
  • Types of information and evidence grades
  • What sources can and can't prove — bias, credibility, recency
  • Evidence capture best practices

AI angle

  • Bounded tasks with explicit source instructions
  • Build a sourcing strategy
  • Run source discovery
  • Prompt for atomic, traceable evidence
  • Flag barriers, gaps, and conflicts

Hands-on activity

  • Build a source map
  • Collect evidence in a structured log
  • Evaluate coverage

Working output

Approved source & collection planStructured evidence logVisible gaps, conflicts & confidence notes
3

Analyze and synthesize

Move from evidence to a defensible intelligence picture and product-relevant implications

Topics

  • Separate evidence, interpretation, and implication
  • Identify table stakes, differentiators, whitespace, and trajectory signals
  • Evaluate workflow fit and user value
  • Surface contradictions, evidence gaps, and segmentation lines
  • Translate findings into decision implications

AI angle

  • Cluster, compare, and surface contradictions
  • Keep evidence separate from model interpretation
  • Prevent model from assuming, overreaching, under-evaluating
  • Require each insight to trace to evidence; preserve ambiguity

Hands-on activity

  • Analyze your evidence using a small number of lenses
  • Build a provisional answer
  • Identify implications for the decision
  • Record what remains uncertain

Working output

Provisional findings & patternsEvidence → interpretation → implication chainInitial answer with confidence & gaps
4

Review, validate, and challenge

Establish whether the answer is sound, defensible, and strong enough to act on

Topics

  • Evidence traceability
  • Can conclusions trace back to evidence?
  • Challenges and Red-teaming

AI angle

  • Fresh-context agents for validation and adversarial review
  • Fail-closed gates before errors propagate

Hands-on activity

  • Validate selected claims against their sources
  • Build the strongest opposing case
  • Identify what would falsify the answer
  • Revise the assessment

Working output

Review & validation findingsRevised assessmentOpposing case & falsification conditions
